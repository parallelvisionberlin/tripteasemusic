The Studio Jams

For more than twenty years, Carlos Salame has been collecting synthesizers, drum machines and strange electronic instruments. Today his studio in Roma, Mexico City houses around 100 different machines.

Some Saturdays each month, the studio opens its doors and becomes a living organism: musicians, friends and guests patching, recording, improvising and listening together. Sometimes up to 60 people are jamming at the same time.

These sessions have become part of Trip Tease’s mythology in La Roma: not a showcase, not a networking night, but a ritual around machines, curiosity and collective energy.

Bio
Carlos Salame aka Trip Tease

Carlos Salame

Carlos Salame is Trip Tease, a producer, DJ and curator from Mexico City whose music moves between electronic romance, disco memory, lo-fi texture and cinematic club language.

Across releases for clipp.art and Astrolead Recordings, plus remixes for artists such as DJ Seinfeld, Trip Tease has built a world that feels intimate and strange: warm machines, emotional fragments, surreal images and dancefloor nostalgia without the obvious retro costume.

Beyond the records, his practice extends into gathering people around sound. His Roma studio jams, built around a large collection of synthesizers and machines, have become a small scene of their own: spontaneous, generous and deeply connected to Mexico City’s creative underground.
