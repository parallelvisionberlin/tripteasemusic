Sex Having Disco

Somewhere between a neon postcard, a forgotten beach club and a machine dreaming in Italian, Sex Having Disco feels like Trip Tease and Alain Vice building their own private mythology of the dancefloor.

The release moves through Italo disco, synth wave, cosmic electronics and late-night club romance without becoming nostalgic theatre. It feels more like a memory of the future: warm basslines, shiny synths, soft danger and that strange glamour of dancing at the edge of the night.

Pt. 2 opens the world further with Milano 2098, featuring Lubó, and a Prins Thomas Diskomiks that stretches the fantasy into a longer, more interstellar trip.

Home Calling

Trip Tease’s remix of DJ Seinfeld’s Home Calling sits inside one of the most elegant remix universes of recent electronic music: MIRRORS (REMIXED), released by Ninja Tune.

The original Mirrors era marked a brighter turn in DJ Seinfeld’s world: less fog, more emotional clarity, bigger melodies and a polished kind of club euphoria. In that context, Trip Tease’s version feels perfectly placed. It does not try to overpower the original. It opens another room inside it.

The remix carries the emotional architecture of Seinfeld’s world but gives it a romantic, late-night pulse: deep, warm, slightly euphoric, with the feeling of walking home after something important happened.

Un, Deux, Trois

Some songs do not ask for a music video. They ask for another dimension.

Created with artist Kris Berle, Un, Deux, Trois expands the visual language surrounding Trip Tease into something suspended between dream logic and animation. Strange landscapes, impossible movements and abstract characters become another instrument, extending the emotional world of the music rather than illustrating it.

It is less a narrative than a visual state of mind, somewhere between surreal cinema, contemporary illustration and electronic hallucination.
