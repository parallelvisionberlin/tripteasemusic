Sex Having Disco

Somewhere between a neon postcard, a forgotten beach club and a machine dreaming in Italian, Sex Having Disco feels like Trip Tease and Alain Vice building their own private mythology of the dancefloor.

The release moves through Italo disco, synth wave, cosmic electronics and late-night club romance without becoming nostalgic theatre. Pt. 2 opens the world further with Milano 2098, featuring Lubó, and a Prins Thomas Diskomiks that stretches the fantasy into a longer, more interstellar trip.

Home Calling

Trip Tease’s remix of DJ Seinfeld’s Home Calling sits inside one of the most elegant remix universes of recent electronic music: MIRRORS (REMIXED), released by Ninja Tune.

The remix carries the emotional architecture of Seinfeld’s world but gives it a romantic, late-night pulse: deep, warm, slightly euphoric, with the feeling of walking home after something important happened.

Studio

The Studio Jams

Trip Tease has been collecting synthesizers for more than twenty years. In Roma, Mexico City, his studio has grown into a living archive of sound: around one hundred synthesizers, drum machines and strange electronic objects gathered slowly over time.

A few Saturdays a month, the studio turns into an open ritual. Friends, musicians and curious strangers arrive, patch cables cross the room, and sometimes up to sixty people are playing, listening or shaping the same jam at once.

It is not a showcase. It is a social instrument: a place where machines become conversation, mistakes become arrangements and the room decides where the music goes.

Bio

Carlos Salame

Carlos Salame is Trip Tease, a Mexican producer from Mexico City whose music blends melodic electronic structures, lo-fi textures, techno pulses and dreamy atmospheres.

Across releases and remixes for labels and projects including clipp.art, Astrolead Recordings and Ninja Tune’s MIRRORS (REMIXED), Trip Tease has shaped a sound that feels intimate but cinematic: club music with a soft-focus memory, analog warmth and a slightly surreal edge.

His world extends beyond records. Through his Roma studio jams, visual collaborations and long relationship with machines, Carlos treats Trip Tease as a whole environment: music, images, people and late-night rituals folded into the same story.

Official profiles describe Trip Tease as the music project of producer Carlos Salame from Mexico City, blending lo-fi and techno sounds with dreamy atmospheres and harmonic structures.
