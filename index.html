Sex Having Disco

Somewhere between a neon postcard, a forgotten beach club and a machine dreaming in Italian, Sex Having Disco feels like Trip Tease and Alain Vice building a private mythology of the dancefloor.

Pt. 2 opens the world further with Milano 2098, featuring Lubó, and a Prins Thomas Diskomiks that stretches the fantasy into a longer, more interstellar trip.

Home Calling

Trip Tease’s remix of DJ Seinfeld’s Home Calling sits inside one of the most elegant remix universes of recent electronic music: MIRRORS (REMIXED), released by Ninja Tune.

A romantic, late-night pulse opens another room inside the song. Deep, warm and slightly euphoric, with the feeling of walking home after something important happened.

Studio

The Studio

Trip Tease has been collecting synthesizers for more than two decades. Today his studio in Roma, Mexico City houses around one hundred different synthesizers and vintage machines.

Every few weeks, the doors open for public jams where up to sixty people create music together at the same time. These sessions have become part of the underground mythology of La Roma: no egos, just machines, curiosity and collective energy.

Bio

Carlos Salame

Carlos Salame is Trip Tease, a producer, DJ and curator from Mexico City. His music moves between electronic romance, disco memory, lo-fi textures and cinematic club language.

Releasing on labels such as clipp.art, Astrolead Recordings and Ninja Tune remix projects, Trip Tease builds a world where images, machines, bodies and late nights belong to the same mythology.

Known for building spaces as much as tracks, Carlos has turned his Roma studio into a meeting point for synthesizer obsessives, artists and friends, a living archive of sound and collaboration.
